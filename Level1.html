Don't Fear the OOP - Beginner


The main plot of the story:
      There is a village called Vaughan. Vaughan has 20 pigs, 40 wolves, and 32 houses.
      There is a male pig named Julien. Julien has a gold-rimmed monocle and a black top hat.
      Mort is a wolf that lives in house number 12.
      In our story Julien starts by eating food and telling everyone how hungry he is, then he goes to blow down Mort’s house.

Every village has a key ingredients: houses, pigs, wolves, and restaurants.
      A standard town has 30 houses and 2 restaurants.

The general idea of the story: The pig gets his revenge on the wolves from “3 Little Pigs”.
      This story takes place in a Village called Vaughan. Vaughan has a population of 20 pigs, 40 wolves and there are 32 houses.

All mammals have a certain number of legs, brains, eyes, a gender, and a house number. They also either have fur or not.
      When asked, they respond with their name.
      A standard mammal starts off with 4 legs, 1 brain, and 2 eyes.

Pigs are based on the idea of mammals.
      They are identical, except that they have some additional qualities, namely a level of agreeableness, a monocle, a hat, a number of wolf houses they’ve blown down, and a level of hunger.

Whenever the pig eats something, the level of hunger goes down by 20%.

If an animal asks the pig how hungry it is, it responds with its level of hunger.

If the pig is supposed to blow a house down, the specified house gets blown down, and add one to the number of houses he has blown down.
      Then print out “Oh my gosh! {the specified house owned by the specified wolf has been blown down}”

Pigs are based on mammals but with additional characteristics: a level of agreeableness, a monocle, a hat, a number of wolf houses they’ve blown down, and a level of hunger.
